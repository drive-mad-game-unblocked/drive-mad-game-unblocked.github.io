Drive Mad

Jump into the exciting world of Drive Mad! Drive cars over crazy paths and beat tough levels.

About Gameplay Rules How to Play Facts

What is Drive Mad?

Drive Mad is a super fun driving game. You control silly cars and trucks on hard paths full of bumps, jumps, and traps. The game makes you laugh when your car flips or crashes. But the goal is to reach the end without failing too much.

Made by Martin Magni, it comes from the Fancade app. You can play it free on websites like Poki, drivemad.com, or apps on phones. It has over 100 levels that get harder each time. Kids and grown-ups love it because it's easy to start but tough to master.

Every level feels new. You drive yellow trucks, monster trucks, or funny cars. The paths have shaky bridges, big ramps, spinning parts, and drops. The game's physics make cars bounce and flip in real ways. This makes wins feel great after many tries.

Drive Mad is big online. People play it at school because it's unblocked on many sites. YouTube has tons of videos showing all levels. Fans make their own versions too!

Gameplay: What Happens in Drive Mad?

The gameplay is simple but exciting. You start at the begin of a short path. Your job is to drive to the flag at the end. But the paths are not flat roads. They twist, turn up, and have holes.

Your car moves with real physics. Go too fast on a ramp, and it flips. Go too slow, and you fall off. You must find the right speed and timing. Each level takes 30 seconds to 2 minutes. But hard ones need 50 tries!

Levels change your car. Level 1 has a basic yellow 4x4 truck. Later, you get long cars, tall ones, or ones with big wheels. This keeps it fresh. No two levels feel the same.

Tip: Watch your car flip in slow motion. It's funny and teaches you what went wrong!

There are sequels like Drive Mad 2 with snow and Drive Mad 3 with more hard paths. They add nitro boosts and new cars to collect.

Play on PC with arrow keys or on phone by tapping sides of screen. It's smooth and works offline in apps.

Rules of Drive Mad

Drive Mad has few rules, but they are key:

  • Reach the finish flag in one go. If your car flips upside down or falls, you lose and restart.
  • No time limit, but faster is better for fun.
  • Go level by level. No skipping in main game, but some sites let you.
  • Keep all wheels down. Balance is everything.
  • Collect nitro if there, but don't crash after boost.
  • Win all 100 levels to beat the game!

No scores or multiplayer in original. Just you vs the path. But fans race times on speedrun.com.

Rule Tip: If car touches water, it sinks. Stay on land!

How to Play Drive Mad Step by Step

Ready to start? Here's how:

  1. Go to drivemad.com, Poki, or download the app.
  2. Click Play. Level 1 loads: "First Gear".
  3. PC: Right arrow or D/X to go forward. Left arrow or A/S/Z to brake/back.
  4. Phone: Tap right screen to speed up, left to slow.
  5. Start slow. Tap gas lightly on bumps.
  6. Feel the physics. Release gas to balance on edges.
  7. Fail? Hit restart. Try again with less speed.
  8. Beat level? Next one unlocks. Enjoy the funny win screen!

Practice on easy levels. Levels 1-20 teach basics. 21-50 add ramps. 51+ are crazy.

Pro Tip: Back up sometimes. Go forward too fast? Reverse and try slow.

For hard levels like 97 or 186, watch YouTube. Communities share tricks.

Cool Facts About Drive Mad

Fact 1: Over 100 levels, but fans add more in Fancade. Some play 200+!
Fact 2: Made in 2022, but based on older "Drive Classic" with 15 levels.
Fact 3: Played on Poki by millions. Top unblocked game at schools.
Fact 4: Cars change per level to keep it fun. No boring same truck.
Fact 5: App has ads, but turn airplane mode for free play.
Fact 6: Speedruns exist. Fastest players do all levels in under 10 minutes!
Fact 7: Inspired games like Eggy Car and Monster Tracks.
Fact 8: Reddit has tips for level 186. Players rage but love it.

Drive Mad shows simple games can be hits. No fancy graphics, just pure fun physics. Play it today and see why everyone talks about it!
